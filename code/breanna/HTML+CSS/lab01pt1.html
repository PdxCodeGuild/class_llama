Khalil (or Kahlil) Gibran is a Lebanese-American writer and poet, most famous for his book the Prophet (first published in the United States in 1923 and since translated into more than 100 languages).
Born in 1883, Gibran spent the earliest years of his life in an Ottoman-ruled village in Lebanon. In 1895, Gibran and his family immigrated to the United States, where they settled in Boston, Massachusetts.
Throughout his life, he lived and/or attended school in the following places:

  • Mount Lebanon Mutasarrifate
  • Boston, Massachusetts
  • Beirut, Lebanon
  • Paris, France
  • New York, New York
